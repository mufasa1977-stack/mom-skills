# Expert Learning Science Canon

Locked 2026-05-23. Tariq's directive: research the science of how humans become experts, then frame the entire Forbidden Files skill+memory system as engineered deliberate practice for an AI agent.

## PART 1 — THE FOUNDATIONAL RESEARCH

### 1. K. Anders Ericsson and Deliberate Practice
Primary: Ericsson, Krampe, Tesch-Römer (1993). Berlin Academy violinists: best ≈ 10,000 hours, good ≈ 8,000, teachers ≈ 4,000 by age 20. The 10,000-hour misinterpretation (Gladwell 2008) was an average, not a threshold. **Four requirements**: (1) specific goal beyond current ability, (2) full focus (90-min blocks, 4-5hr/day max), (3) immediate informative feedback, (4) reflection + refinement. Most "experience" is not practice — radiologist with 20yr unstructured reads worse than 5yr with second-reads (Ericsson 2004).

### 2. Dreyfus Skill Acquisition Stages
Novice (rules) → Advanced Beginner (situational rules) → Competent (conscious planning + emotional ownership) → Proficient (intuitive grasp + analytic action) → Expert (intuitive both). Transitions are emotional, not just cognitive.

### 3. Chunking + Expert Memory (Chase-Simon 1973)
Chess masters: 93% board reconstruction on game-states, 30% on random. Chunks = patterns, not pieces. Estimated 50,000-300,000 chunks in expert LTM (Gobet-Simon 1996). Retrieved into working memory in <200ms. **Chunks are domain-specific and non-transferable** — chess world champion fails at Go without dedicated hours. Implication for AI: skill files ARE Claude's chunk library; trigger phrases are the retrieval mechanism.

### 4. Mental Models — Charlie Munger's Latticework
USC Harvard Law 1995 speech. 80-90 deeply understood models from physics (compound interest, critical mass), biology (evolution), psychology (25 cognitive biases), math (Bayes, decision trees), economics (incentives, scale), engineering (margin of safety, redundancy). The latticework: each model alone is partial; many models intersecting on the same problem yield triangulated answer. "Man with a hammer syndrome" = reasoning with one model.

**First principles** (Aristotle / Descartes / Feynman / Musk): strip problem to physical/economic truths, rebuild from there. Musk battery example: $600/kWh analogy → $80/kWh raw materials → built the 92% delta.

**Cross-domain transfer via mental models** — the ONE form of transfer that survives domain specificity. Not chess patterns; abstract structural patterns. Compound interest applies to money + bacterial growth + reputation + skill + codebase complexity.

### 5. Polymath Learning Frameworks
Isaacson biographies (Da Vinci, Einstein, Franklin, Jobs, Doudna): five-trait pattern = boundary-disrespecting curiosity + observation rigor + synthesis instinct + tolerance for unfinished work + notebook habit. E.O. Wilson Consilience: unity of knowledge across disciplines; breakthroughs at seams. T-shaped vs comb-shaped vs M-shaped knowledge. Epstein Range: kind environments reward specialization (chess, golf, music); wicked environments reward generalists who sampled many fields (medicine, creative work, AI documentary production).

### 6. Spaced Repetition + Memory Consolidation
Ebbinghaus 1885: 50% forgotten in 1hr, 70% in 24hr, 80% in a month without rehearsal. Each successful retrieval flattens the curve. SuperMemo SM-2: expanding intervals 1, 6, 16, 35, 90+ days based on recall difficulty. **Active recall beats passive review** — Karpicke-Roediger 2008: 4x re-read worse than 1x read + 3x test. Re-reading creates fluency illusion. Sleep-mediated consolidation: REM replay + slow-wave hippocampus-to-cortex transfer (Stickgold 2005, Walker 2017).

### 7. Interleaving + Variation
Kornell-Bjork 2008: interleaved practice feels harder but beats blocked by 40-100% on delayed discrimination tests. The hard part of expertise isn't execution — it's problem identification, which interleaving forces every rep.

### 8. Generation Effect (Slamecka-Graf 1978)
Self-generated information remembered 30-50% better than read information. **Feynman Technique**: pick concept → explain to a child → notice where explanation breaks → return to source → re-explain until smooth. The child's-vocabulary constraint strips analogical hand-waving.

### 9. Feedback Loops (Hattie-Timperley 2007)
Good feedback = immediate + specific + actionable + goal-referenced + external/objective. **Tariq's role IS structurally a deliberate-practice coach** — watches the rep, names the gap, demands corrective skill.

### 10. Meta-Learning + Theory of Constraints (Goldratt 1984)
Improvement comes from finding + removing the single binding constraint. Most learners stop at object level; experts spend ~10% of time at meta level — debugging their own learning process.

## PART 2 — GENIUS CASE STUDIES

- **Mozart**: father Leopold's letters show 4-5hr/day structured drills from age 4. ~17 years before first masterworks.
- **Tiger Woods**: Earl began structured drills at age 2. Video review + 9-shot drill = canonical kind-environment example.
- **Feynman**: notebooks at Caltech archive — every concept reformulated in own notation 3-4x. Separate notebook "Things I Don't Know" revisited yearly.
- **Da Vinci**: 7,200+ notebook pages. Mirror writing. Same subject revisited decades (water flow 40+ years). Notebook as external working memory.
- **Munger**: 80% of waking hours reading. Internal mental-model checklists run on every decision.
- **Musk**: rocket cost decomposition (2002), battery decomposition (2008), tunnel boring (2017). Refuse industry analogy, rebuild from physical constraints.

## PART 3 — THE 10X EFFECT IS REAL

Sackman-Erikson-Grant 1968: 20:1 spread on debug time, 10:1 on code size, 5:1 on execution speed across professional programmers. Five drivers: (1) pattern recognition speed, (2) larger working chunks, (3) better mental models, (4) faster feedback cycles, (5) better problem identification.

## PART 4 — GENIUS = RECOMBINATION (Steven Johnson 2010)

The slow hunch (Darwin assembled natural selection from Malthus + Lyell + pigeon-breeders + Beagle observations over 25 years). Adjacent possible (Kauffman) — next-step innovations bounded by what already exists. Cross-pollination — Royal Society (1660), Lunar Society of Birmingham (1765 — Watt, Wedgwood, Erasmus Darwin, Priestley), Bell Labs cafeteria, MIT Building 20.

## PART 5 — GENIUS HABITS

Notebook keeping (Feynman, Da Vinci, Edison 3,500 notebooks, Darwin, Newton) = external working memory. Long walks (Darwin's Sandwalk 4-5 loops/day, Jobs, Beethoven) — default-mode-network activation, 4mph correlates with peak insight rate (Oppezzo-Schwartz 2014). Conversation with smart peers. Wide reading beyond domain. Sleep + dreams (Mendeleev periodic table 1869, Kekulé benzene ring, Loewi acetylcholine, McCartney "Yesterday").

## PART 6 — ANTI-GENIUS PATTERNS

Local optima trap. Sunk-cost. Confirmation bias. Premature optimization (Knuth 1974). "I already know" plateau. Fluency illusion. Tool worship. One-hammer syndrome (Munger).
